Equality is Not a Favor; It is a Right

Ronna Jane G. Zabala

In this world where we thrive on diversity and connectedness, gender equality is not just a high aspiration; it is a cornerstone of a fair and just society. We often hear the word “equality,” but what does it truly mean in our daily lives? It means that every person, regardless of their gender, should have equal rights, opportunities, and respect. Most importantly, no one should be left behind because of who they truly are.

Gender Equality is not just about comparing men and women or putting one gender over another. It is about balancing and appreciating the value of every person. Over the past years, stereotypes created some barriers that continue to affect employment, education, leadership, and others. Breaking down these barriers is not just a women’s issue, it is a human issue.

When all genders have equal access, the community will become more healthier, peaceful, and more prepared to face the future. When all genders are given an equal chance to lead, societies will become so much better. When we normalize women to work instead of being housewives, men can do household responsibilities and can express their feelings without the feeling of being judged by others. And when the members of the LGBTQ+ community are well respected and included, the world will become stronger and lead to true diversity.

It is not easy to achieve gender equality. It calls us to confront injustices and oppose unfair systems. However, awareness is the first step towards progress, which will continue with any action, no matter how big or small. Every effort matters, whether supporting inclusive policies or paying attention to every unheard voice.

We must keep in mind that equality does not mean that there is special treatment. It is not a favor that is granted. It is a universal right that ought to be protected, lived, and practiced in our daily lives. Believing in equality is not enough, we also need to take action on it.

Society does not only develop but thrives when all genders are treated fairly and equally.

Equality is not just a favor; it is a right. And the world needs us to stand and speak up for it.
